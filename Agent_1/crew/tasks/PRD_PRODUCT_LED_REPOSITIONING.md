## 1. The Insight

Nobody wakes up wanting "facial analysis." But people DO wake up thinking:
- "Mum's birthday is in 2 weeks"
- "Family holiday next month — need something fun for the kids"
- "New baby — who does she look like?"

Right now FamiliLook leads with the process ("upload photos for biometric analysis") instead of the outcome ("make a mug that shows your bond with Mum"). The AI engine is a strength but it's being sold as the product. It should be invisible infrastructure — like how Moonpig doesn't lead with "our printing pipeline."

## 2. The Shift

| Before (Process-led) | After (Product-led) |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| "Upload 3 photos" | "Make a mug for Dad's birthday" |
| "See which parent you take after" | "72% your Mum's eyes — on a mug she'll keep" |
| "Consent to biometric processing" | "Upload a photo of you and Mum to create your gift" |
| Analysis is the destination | Analysis is a step in making the product |
| Products discovered AFTER results | Products are the entry point |
| Subscription gates core feature | Per-product pricing visible upfront |

### Key Design Decisions

1. **Products first, process second.** The 2x2 product grid is above the fold. "How it works" is below — for users who need reassurance, not as the primary pitch.

2. **Occasion banner is dynamic.** Uses the existing campaign date system (Mother's Day, Easter, etc.) to show the most relevant upcoming occasion with a direct CTA.

3. **Prices visible upfront.** No "sign up to see pricing." Per-product pricing removes the subscription barrier for first purchase. Subscription becomes the value-after-first-purchase play: "Liked your mug? Get 10% off everything with Plus."

4. **Free games as acquisition.** FamiliUno and Poker are free entry points that require photos but no payment. Once someone has played with their family's faces, they're primed to buy the physical deck or a keepsake.

5. **Social proof is product-focused.** Not "the AI was accurate" but "my mum cried when she saw it" — emotional outcomes, not technical credibility.

### Three Front Doors, One Engine

The homepage serves three distinct audiences with different intent:

1. **"Make something" audience** (gift buyers, occasion shoppers)
   - Entry: Product cards with prices → upload 2-3 photos → analysis (invisible) → product preview → buy
   - Motivation: They want the product. Analysis is how the product gets personalised.
   - Photos: 2-3 (you + parent, or you + parent + child)

2. **"Find out" audience** (curious families, viral share recipients)
   - Entry: "Who do I look like?" → upload 3 photos (2 parents + you) → analysis → results → share/play
   - Motivation: They want the answer. Products are a natural next step after emotional result.
   - Photos: 3 (mum + dad + child)

3. **"How well do you match?" audience** (couples, friends, siblings)
   - Entry: "How well do you match?" → upload 2 photos → instant result → share
   - Motivation: Fun, social, shareable. Lowest friction of all three doors.
   - Photos: 2 only (lowest barrier)
   - Uses: FamiliMatch Solo (desktop6) or `/compare/faces` endpoint
   - Viral hook: "89% match — we've got the same eyes!" → share → friend tries it
   - Monetisation: Product cards after result ("Put your 89% match on a mug")

All three paths converge at a results page (kinship results for doors 1-2, match results for door 3). Each results page shows:
- Feature breakdown (the answer they came for)
- Occasion-aware product cards (the monetisation moment)
- Games strip (the retention/virality moment)

**No path is buried or secondary.** All three are visible on the homepage. Product cards are first (highest intent = highest conversion), discovery CTA is second (lower barrier = higher volume), match CTA is third (lowest friction = highest viral potential). Door 3 routes to FamiliMatch (desktop6) or a future `/match` route in desktop2 — the exact routing is a Phase C decision.

## 5. Product-Driven Upload Flow

### Current: Intent → Upload → Analysis → Results → Discover products
### Proposed: Product → Upload → Analysis (invisible) → Product preview → Buy

When a user taps "Make it →" on a mug:

```
1. Product page: "Bond Mug — £14.99"
   Shows mockup, features, dimensions
   CTA: "Upload photos to create yours"
   
2. Upload (2 photos only — you + parent)
   Slot 1: "Upload a photo of you"
   Slot 2: "Upload a photo of Mum"
   Context: "We'll discover which features you share"
   
3. Processing (10-30 seconds)
   "Creating your mug..."
   Progress bar + rotating fun facts
   
4. Product preview with YOUR results baked in
   The mug mockup now shows YOUR actual bond percentage
   "72% match — got Mum's eyes, smile, and face shape"
   8-feature breakdown shown ON the product
   
5. Buy
   "Add to basket — £14.99"
   "Download preview (free)"
   "Make another product with this result"
```

**The analysis never appears as a standalone page.** It's embedded in the product creation flow. The results ARE the product.

## 7. Pricing Strategy Shift

### Current: Subscription-first
- Free tier: 2 analyses, watermarked previews, can't order
- Plus (£3.99/mo): Unlimited analyses + ordering
- Problem: Users must subscribe BEFORE they know if the product is worth it

### Proposed: Product-first, subscription-second
- **First product: no subscription required.** Pay per product (£3.99–£39.99).
- **Subscription pitch AFTER first purchase:** "Loved your mug? Get Plus for 10% off everything + unlimited analyses."
- Free tier still gets: 2 free analyses, digital games, shareable PNG
- This removes the "I have to subscribe to even try" barrier

**Implementation note:** This requires backend changes to allow keepsake ordering on Free tier (currently blocked by `canOrderMerchandise()`). The subscription discount still applies for Plus/Pro.

## 9. Archive Policy (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

The current homepage layout (`HomePage.jsx`), results page layout (`MobileResultsSection.jsx`, `MobileResultsCarousel.jsx`), and `ProductShelf.jsx` must NOT be deleted when implementing the new layouts.

**Approach:**
- Current components stay in the codebase, intact
- New components (`OccasionShelf.jsx`, new homepage layout) are ADDITIVE
- A feature flag (`VITE_PRODUCT_LED_HOMEPAGE`) controls which layout renders
- Flag OFF = current layout (exact same as today, zero change)
- Flag ON = new product-led layout
- This allows instant rollback without a deploy — just flip the flag in Vercel

**Files that must be preserved (read-only, not modified):**
- `src/pages/HomePage.jsx` — current homepage
- `src/components/results/ProductShelf.jsx` — current product shelf
- `src/layout/MobileResultsSection.jsx` — current results layout
- `src/components/results/MobileResultsCarousel.jsx` — current results carousel

**New files (additive):**
- `src/components/keepsakes/OccasionShelf.jsx` — new occasion-aware product cards
- `src/pages/HomePageProductLed.jsx` — new product-led homepage (Phase B)

The parent components that render these (likely `AppLayout.jsx` or route config) will use a conditional:
```jsx
{import.meta.env.VITE_PRODUCT_LED_HOMEPAGE === "true" 
  ? <HomePageProductLed /> 
  : <HomePage />}
```

Same pattern for OccasionShelf vs ProductShelf on the results page.

## 10. Quality & Rigour Framework (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

This framework is mandatory for every phase. It was forged from real incidents during the Smart Photo Selection sprints (2026-04-01 to 2026-04-02):

- **TDZ errors** from referencing variables before definition (2026-04-02)
- **7 test failures** from missing mock exports when source imports changed (2026-04-02)
- **Green badge contradiction** from `/detect` and `/kinship/analyze` using different code paths (2026-04-02)
- **.env.local flags leaking** into test runs, making "flag OFF" tests pass falsely (2026-04-02)
- **Direct code edits** bypassing agent discipline, causing compounding failures (2026-04-02)

### Rule 1: Phase 0 Baseline Tests BEFORE Any Feature Code

Before modifying ANY existing component, the qa_lead agent MUST deliver baseline tests that lock in the current working behaviour. This was proven essential when PhotoSlot tests caught the missing `enhanceQualityMessage` mock.

For this PRD, Phase 0 must cover:
- `ProductShelf.jsx` — current rendering, product ranking, CTA behaviour
- `MobileResultsSection.jsx` — current results layout, carousel, product shelf integration
- `HomePage.jsx` — current homepage rendering, CTAs, navigation
- Campaign/occasion detection system (wherever it lives)
- `canOrderMerchandise()` / plan gating logic in usePlanFeatures.js

**Minimum: 30 baseline tests locking existing behaviour.**

### Rule 2: Feature Flags Gate Every Phase

Every phase uses a feature flag. Flag OFF = zero change to existing behaviour. Flag can be killed in Vercel env vars in under 5 minutes.

| Phase | Flag |
|-------|------|
| A (OccasionShelf) | `VITE_OCCASION_SHELF` |
| B (Homepage) | `VITE_PRODUCT_LED_HOMEPAGE` |
| C (Product creation flow) | `VITE_PRODUCT_CREATION_FLOW` |

When flag OFF, the original component renders. The flag check happens in the PARENT component (not inside the new component) so the new component is never even imported when flag is off (tree-shaking removes it).

### Rule 3: Blast Radius Audit Per Phase

Before writing feature code, the qa_lead agent must produce a blast radius report:
- Every file that imports the component being replaced/wrapped
- Every state/context value that the new component will read
- Every localStorage key that could be affected
- Every downstream consumer (keepsakes, games, analytics, pairwise)
- Every E2E flow that touches the modified area

This was proven essential when the FaceNamingSheet TDZ error was caused by referencing `faces` (line 2626) from handlers defined at line 2293.

### Rule 4: Agent-Only Code Changes

Claude must NOT edit source code directly. ALL code changes delegated to agents. Direct edits caused 7 test failures on 2026-04-02 when mock updates were missed. Agents read full file context before editing.

**Exception:** Claude can read files, run tests, run builds, and commit. But the Edit/Write tools on source files go through agents only.

### Rule 5: Three-Tier Post-Deploy Verification

Every phase follows the same verification protocol:

**Tier 1 — Automated (<10 minutes post-deploy)**
- Unit tests pass (flag OFF AND flag ON)
- Build succeeds, bundle size within limit
- E2E smoke test passes
- No new console errors

**Tier 2 — Manual (same day, <2 hours)**
- Test on real devices (iOS Safari, Android Chrome, Desktop Chrome)
- Full flow with flag OFF (existing behaviour unchanged)
- Full flow with flag ON (new behaviour works)
- Test both from homepage AND from direct URL
- Test with Free tier, Plus tier, and Pro tier

**Tier 3 — Analytics (24-48 hours)**
- Growth Monitor agent reviews: events firing correctly?
- No drop in existing metrics (page_view, analysis_complete)
- New events appearing (occasion_card_clicked, product_view, etc.)
- Upload conversion not regressed

### Rule 6: Archive First, Replace Second

Current components are NEVER deleted. New components are additive. A conditional in the parent switches between old and new based on the feature flag. This means instant rollback.

The old component stays in the codebase, importable, renderable. After 30 days with the new component stable, the old one can be removed in a cleanup PR.

### Rule 7: Test What Ships, Not What You Hope

Every test must verify the ACTUAL rendered output, not mock assumptions:
- If a product card should show "72% Mum — on a mug", test that the rendered DOM contains those exact words with the actual data
- If an occasion banner should appear 39 days before Mother's Day, test with a mocked date 39 days before and verify the banner renders
- If "flag OFF" should show the old ProductShelf, render with flag off and verify ProductShelf's content appears (not just "something renders")

### Rule 8: No Broken Windows

If a test fails during development, STOP. Fix the test before writing more code. Do not accumulate failures. The 7-failure cascade on 2026-04-02 happened because the first failure (missing mock) was not fixed before more changes were layered on.

### Rule 9: Commit After Each Phase, Not After All Phases

Each phase gets its own commit with:
- Phase 0 tests (separate commit)
- Phase feature code + feature tests (one commit)
- Verification results documented in change_log.md

Never batch multiple phases into one commit. If a phase needs reverting, only that phase's commit is affected.

### Rule 10: Pre-Edit Checklist Still Applies

Per CLAUDE.md, the mandatory pre-edit checklist applies to every file modification:
1. Validate scope (`python .claude/validate_scope.py`)
2. Show diff preview
3. Wait for approval
4. Apply edit (via agent)
5. Log the change
6. Run tests

YouVersion is the gold standard for how FamiliLook should feel to navigate. Key principles to match:

- **Card-based layout** — content presented in clean, elevated cards with consistent spacing and rounded corners
- **Bottom tab navigation** — persistent, intuitive, icon-led. The user always knows where they are.
- **Content IS the interface** — minimal chrome, no decorative UI that doesn't serve the content. The product cards, results, and photos should dominate the screen.
- **Progressive disclosure** — simple surface, depth on tap. Homepage shows 3-4 hero products. "Browse all" reveals the rest. No overwhelming grids.
- **Smooth transitions** — page transitions, card flips, and scroll behaviour should feel native (iOS-like), not web-like. Use `scroll-snap`, `transform` animations, momentum scrolling.
- **Information hierarchy** — the most important thing on each screen is immediately obvious. No competing CTAs. One primary action per viewport.
- **Mobile-first native feel** — FamiliLook must feel like an app, not a website. 44px touch targets. No tiny text. No horizontal scroll accidents. Thumb-reachable primary actions.

**Test:** Would a user mistake this for a native iOS app? If not, it needs more work.

#### Product Quality Benchmark: Cornish Prints UK

Cornish Prints UK is the standard our product presentation must match. Products should look premium, not novelty.

- **Product mockups must look like gallery pieces** — the mug, print, or card should look like something from a curated gift shop, not a novelty stall
- **Clean, minimal product photography** — white or neutral backgrounds, proper lighting, no clutter. The product is the hero.
- **Premium materials feel** — copy should reference quality: "museum-quality giclee", "324gsm card stock", "dishwasher-safe ceramic". These details build trust.
- **No gimmick aesthetic** — the AI personalisation should ELEVATE the product ("your family bond, beautifully printed") not cheapen it ("wacky AI face mug!!")
- **Frame-worthy results** — every physical product should look like something the recipient would want to display, not hide in a drawer

**Test:** Would Cornish Prints stock this? If the product mockup looks cheap next to their catalogue, it needs redesigning.
