---

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE

1. Start with **Mama Bear** (prompt 1). She sets the style anchor for all other characters.
2. Once you have a Mama Bear you like, generate the rest of the family in the same session/thread so DALL-E maintains style consistency.
3. Each prompt is self-contained — paste the entire block. Do NOT include the `[STYLE LOCK PREFIX]` placeholder; it's already baked into each prompt.
4. After generating, if a character drifts from the style, add: "Match the exact same art style as the previous character — same outline weight, same eye style, same proportions."
5. Generate each at **1024x1024** on a **white background**.

## PROMPT 1: MAMA BEAR — `mama_proud` (Generate First — Style Anchor)

```
Create a single cartoon character illustration on a plain white background. This character is called "Mama Bear" and she is the mother figure in a family of illustrated mascots for a baby resemblance app called FamiliLook. She will be printed on ceramic mugs alongside real family photos, so she needs to feel warm, approachable, and gift-worthy — like something a new mum would proudly display on her desk.

STYLE RULES (apply to this and ALL future characters in this family):
- Art style sits between Mr. Men (simple geometric shapes) and Duolingo's owl (expressive, contemporary, rounded). Think Pusheen's simplicity with more character.
- FLAT COLOUR FILLS ONLY. Absolutely no gradients, no shading, no shadows, no highlights on the body, no 3D effects, no texture. Every coloured region is a single solid colour.
- Thick, uniform-width dark outlines around every shape. The outline weight is consistent everywhere — same thickness on the head as on the arms as on the props. Outlines have rounded endpoints and rounded joins (no sharp corners anywhere).
- The character is built from simple geometric shapes: circles, ovals, rounded rectangles, pear shapes. No complex organic forms.
- Background is pure white with nothing else — no ground line, no shadow, no decorative elements, no environment.

MAMA BEAR ANATOMY:
- HEAD: A wide circle, slightly wider than tall (like a squashed circle). This is approximately 40% of the total character height. Positioned centrally at the top.
- HAIR: A perfectly round bun sitting directly on top of the head circle, like a ball balanced on the head. The bun is about 30% the diameter of the head. Filled in dark plum colour (#5A2035).
- EYES: Two simple solid black dots (#1A1A1A), each with a single tiny white circle highlight in the upper-right area. The eyes are positioned in the middle third of the head, spaced apart by roughly one eye-width. NO eyelids, NO iris detail, NO pupil rings — just a solid dot with a white highlight.
- LASHES: Above each eye, two short thick curved strokes sweeping upward and outward — like parentheses tipped on their side. These are the key feature that distinguishes Mama from Papa. Same dark plum colour as the outline.
- MOUTH: A single upward-curving line (a smile). No teeth showing. No lips. Just one confident curved stroke, about 60% the width of the eye spacing. Positioned in the lower third of the head.
- CHEEKS: Two small solid circles of soft pink (#E8637A) at about 40% opacity, one on each side of the face, positioned between the eyes and the mouth vertically, and outboard of the eyes horizontally.
- BODY: A pear shape or bell shape — narrow at the top (where it meets the neck area below the head), widening smoothly to a broad rounded bottom. The body is about 55% of the total character height. Filled in rose pink (#C0364E). The transition from head to body is seamless — no visible neck, the head sits directly on the body.
- ARMS: Two short, thick, rounded tube shapes extending from the upper sides of the body. They end in mitten hands (rounded paddle shapes with no individual fingers, a simple thumb bump is optional). The RIGHT arm (from our perspective) is bent with the mitten hand resting on the hip. The LEFT arm is extended slightly forward, holding a tea cup.
- LEGS: Two very short stumpy cylinders at the bottom of the bell body, barely poking out. Simple rounded feet at the bottom. The legs are understated — the bell body does most of the visual grounding.
- TEA CUP: A simple cylindrical mug shape held in the left mitten hand. The mug is a small rounded rectangle with a C-shaped handle on one side. Two small wavy lines rise from the top (steam). Mug fill: soft pink (#E8637A). Steam lines: light mauve (#C9A0AC).

POSE: Standing tall and confident. Body angled very slightly to the right (about 5-10 degrees, not straight-on). Weight evenly distributed. Head facing the viewer. She looks proud, warm, and self-assured — like a mum who just found out her baby looks exactly like her.

COLOUR PALETTE (exact hex values):
- Body fill: #C0364E (rose pink)
- Head fill: #C0364E (same as body — head and body are one continuous colour)
- Hair bun fill: #5A2035 (dark plum)
- All outlines: #5A2035 (dark plum), uniform thickness throughout
- Eye dots: #1A1A1A (near-black)
- Eye highlights: #FFFFFF (white)
- Lash strokes: #5A2035 (dark plum)
- Mouth stroke: #5A2035 (dark plum)
- Cheek circles: #E8637A at 40% opacity
- Tea cup: #E8637A (soft pink)
- Steam lines: #C9A0AC (light mauve)

SIZE AND COMPOSITION:
- The character fills about 70-75% of the square canvas vertically, centred horizontally.
- There should be clear breathing room above the bun and below the feet.
- The character should feel balanced and stable — not floating, not cramped.

DO NOT INCLUDE: Any text, any background elements, any ground/floor, any shadow beneath the character, any gradient, any environment, any other characters. Just the single character on white.
```

## POST-PROCESSING PIPELINE

After generating all characters as raster PNGs:

1. **Clean up**: Remove any background artefacts. Ensure pure white background. Remove any AI hallucinations (extra shapes, doubled lines).
2. **Vectorise**: Use Adobe Illustrator Image Trace (High Fidelity Photo preset, limit to 10-12 colours) or Vectorizer.ai (web-based, excellent with flat cartoons).
3. **Organise SVG layers**: Rename groups to: `props-back`, `body`, `body-detail`, `arms`, `head`, `hair`, `face`, `glasses`, `props-front`, `occasion-overlay`, `speech-bubble`.
4. **Replace colours with CSS variables**: Swap hardcoded hex fills with `.fill-primary`, `.fill-light`, `.fill-dark` etc. classes mapped to CSS custom properties. This enables runtime theme switching.
5. **Add accessibility**: `role="img"`, `<title>`, `<desc>`, `aria-label` on each SVG.
6. **Optimise**: Run through SVGO. Target: under 8KB per character SVG.
7. **Validate**: Check all 7 themes render correctly, character is recognisable at 40x40px thumbnail size.

File naming: `mama_proud.svg`, `papa_cheeky.svg`, `cub_sleeping.svg`, `mini_celebrating.svg`, `gran_laughing.svg`, `gramps_showing_off.svg`
